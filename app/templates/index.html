Cavity

A dental cavity, also known as tooth decay or caries, is a hole that forms in the tooth when the enamel is damaged by acids. These acids are produced by bacteria in your mouth when they feed on sugar from the food and drinks you consume.

Over time, the cavity can grow deeper, reaching the softer layers of the tooth and eventually the nerve, causing pain, sensitivity, and infection.
